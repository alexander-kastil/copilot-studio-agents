# Northwind Traders objection responses

Standard responses for the objection-handling skill, grouped around the three recurring drivers: price, timing, and competitors. Use these as the value points when reframing a customer push-back. Confirm any figure with the account owner before sending.

| Objection | What the buyer usually means | Recommended response |
|-----------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| "Your price is higher than the others." | They are comparing sticker price, not total cost. | Shift to total cost of ownership: bundled onboarding, no per-seat overage fees, and a fixed annual rate for three years. Offer a side-by-side cost sheet. |
| "We do not have budget this quarter." | Timing, not value; they need to defer the spend. | Propose a scoped pilot now with a start-of-next-quarter rollout, so evaluation happens before the spend lands. |
| "A competitor offers the same thing cheaper." | They see the products as interchangeable. | Name the Northwind differentiators: 99.9 percent delivery reliability, a named account manager, and same-region support. Offer a reference call with a similar customer. |
| "We are happy with our current supplier." | Low urgency to switch away from the incumbent. | Do not attack the incumbent. Ask what one thing they wish worked better, then map a single Northwind strength to that gap. |
| "Send me some information and I will look later." | A soft brush-off to end the call. | Agree to send one focused one-pager, then book a 15-minute follow-up while still on the call so the thread stays warm. |
| "We need to think about it." | An unspoken concern is blocking them. | Acknowledge, then ask which part needs the most thought (price, fit, or timing) so the real objection surfaces. |
